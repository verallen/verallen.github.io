Intro

Veronica Allen is a 4th year undergraduate student at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. She is majoring in Geospatial Science and Technology and has a minor in hispanic studies. Her anticipated graduation date is May 2020.

Other than school, Veronica is involved with club tennis, the Reformed University Fellowship, and the Center for Sport, Peace and Society. The spring of her junior year, she studied abroad in Madrid, Spain for five months where she attended a local university and lived with a host family.

Resumé

Projects

My project with the Center for Sport, Peace and Society is to create an interactive map that our clients can use to display data that they have collected on gender equality in sports around the world. The main goal was to have an easily-accessible and user-friendly map that allows delegates from countries near and far to evaluate the legal framework within sports in surrounding areas. This would create inspiration and more space to bring equality and make a difference in their own communities.

My project for an intermediate GIS course was aimed to provide a better understanding of public transportation in DC and how accessible it is to the homeless population. I created a map that relays the amount of transit stops available within half a mile of certain homeless shelters.
